Understanding the Metrics
EV Stock Share Primary
EVs as a percentage of all vehicles currently on the road — the main comparison metric.
EV Stock
Total number of EVs in the fleet (cumulative count of vehicles on the road).
EV Sales Share
New EVs as a percentage of all new vehicles sold in a given year.
EV Sales
Absolute number of new EVs sold in a given year.
Why stock share? Absolute numbers favor large countries like China over adoption leaders like Norway. Stock share reveals true electrification progress regardless of population or fleet size.

Understanding Policy Data

Category
● Taxation — Fiscal incentives, tax credits, rebates, fee exemptions.
● LDV — Light-duty vehicles — cars, SUVs, passenger vehicles.
● M/HDV — Medium & heavy-duty — trucks and commercial fleets.
● Multiple — Policy spanning multiple vehicle categories.
● Manufacturing — Production, supply-side, and industrial policy.
● EVSE — EV Supply Equipment — charging infrastructure policy.
● Bus — Bus-specific electrification policy.
● 2/3W — Two & three wheelers — scooters, motorcycles, rickshaws.
Type
● Legislation — Enacted laws and regulations
● Announced Target — Official government goals
● Objective — Stated policy aims
● Proposal — Under consideration
Level
● Supranational — e.g., EU-level directives
● Multi-national — Multiple countries
● National — Country-wide policies
● Subnational — State, province, or city

The density paradox: As EV adoption accelerates, chargers-per-1,000-EVs naturally declines — not because infrastructure shrinks, but because fleet growth outpaces it. This declining ratio is the signature of a successful infrastructure-led market.
